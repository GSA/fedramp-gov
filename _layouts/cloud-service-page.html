Partnering with FedRAMP

The federal government is one of the largest buyers of cloud technology, and Cloud Service Providers (CSPs) offer agencies innovative products that help them save money and time while meeting their critical mission needs.

CSPs who have a Cloud Service Offering (CSO) that is being used by the federal government should be thinking about obtaining a FedRAMP Authorization. Since FedRAMP applies a common security framework for all cloud products and services, CSPs only need to go through the FedRAMP authorization process one time for each product, saving time and money while still adhering to federal security requirements. Additionally, FedRAMP is not only recognized government-wide, but internationally, commercially, in higher education, and within state and local governments as the leader in security frameworks. The FedRAMP PMO provides training, guidance, and advisory support to cloud service providers, helping them navigate the FedRAMP process and understand the requirements.

How FedRamp Can Help

FedRAMP creates a partnership between the federal government and industry. There are many benefits and opportunities of pursuing a FedRAMP authorization for CSPs and the PMO can provide guidance around best practices and strategies to becoming FedRAMP Authorized.

CSP Authorization Playbook

The CSP Authorization Playbook: Getting Started with FedRAMP provides an overview of all of the partners involved in a FedRAMP authorization, things CSPs should consider when determining their authorization strategy, the types of authorizations, and important considerations for their CSOs when working with FedRAMP.

Marketplace Designations for CSPs

FedRAMP Marketplace Designations for Cloud Service Providers outlines the requirements for FedRAMP designations on the FedRAMP Marketplace for CSPs, including achieving a designation for a CSO, maintaining a designation, and the removal of a designation.

Security Controls Baseline

The FedRAMP Security Controls Baseline provides the catalog of FedRAMP High, Moderate, Low, and Tailored LI-SaaS baseline security controls, along with additional guidance and requirements.

FedRAMP’s Training page

FedRAMP’s Training page has a path specifically for CSPs, which is designed to help CSPs understand the requirements of security package development and give a detailed overview of the required templates and their supporting documentation.
