Authorization for Low-Impact Software-as-a-Service (LI-SaaS)

The FedRAMP Tailored Baseline for Cloud Service Providers (CSPs) with Low-Impact Software-as-a-Service (LI-SaaS) Systems was developed to support industry solutions that are deemed as low risk by the agencies who are using these services.

Benefits of FedRAMP Tailored

Creates a faster and streamlined process for systems that are low risk for use such as collaboration tools, project management applications, and tools that help develop open-source code

Supports government Authorizing Officialsâ€™ (AO) need for a standardized approach for determining the risks associated with low-impact cloud applications

Incorporates industry input in order to provide the government with the agility to leverage valuable services while maintaining appropriate security levels

FedRAMP was originally built around enterprise-wide solutions that would cover the broadest range of data types for cloud architectures moving into the federal space. FedRAMP currently has three sets of baseline security requirements: Low, Moderate, and High impact based on FIPS 199 categorization.

The FedRAMP Tailored baseline provides a minimum set of security control requirements. As always and required by law, agency AOs have the ultimate responsibility of determining if additional security controls are required to remain in compliance with agency-specific policies, procedures, and their own risk tolerance. The FedRAMP program, including our goals for Tailored, is a key part of issuing an informed, risk-based Authority to Operate (ATO).
